About

I’m an African - American multidisciplinary artist who practice consists of narrative paintings of BIPOC and the LGBTQ+ community, digital drawings, community art projects, photography and many more.

I am a first generation college graduate who studied computer science and art which led to my interests in the relationship between technology and art and other people’s experiences with it. I focus on giving a voice to a community or an important topic through my work with my art and UI designs in order to help people.

About

When I was a toddler, I would draw on the walls especially the television screen because I loved the static feel. I always knew I was going to be an artist as that was the main way I expressed myself throughout my life....

I use visual and digital art to express myself and learn ways how to positively impact my communities as an African and American woman. I strive to use my creative mind to enforce new ideologies that society forces others to not think about. Hopefully, I will continue to change the negative narrative on the African diaspora, Africa as a continent, and other minority communities.

While focusing on Studio Art and Computer Science at Brandeis University, I will find ways to expand my network, learn about how black creatives navigate the work industry, and practice and learn more about my interests.
